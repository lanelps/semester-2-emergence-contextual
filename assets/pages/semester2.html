Stencil:

Gathered visual research material from last semester showing emergent properties and behaviours found in nature, treated/ filtered to binary colors of just black and white (meaning the image only has black or white, on or off, 1 or 0) relating back to this idea of simple rules making up a complex system. Ripple:

Drawing from my found imagery, looking at repeating patterns and layers and applying it to letter forms. 3D Text:

Looking at the idea of taking a simple rule and applying it multiple times to a single object.
First example, taking a 3 dimensional form of the letter "A" and rotating it around its central axis.
Also playing with incremental scale, and also treating the form of the letter as if it were made of tiny particles. S.E.M:

Gathered visual imagery taken with a Scanning Electron Microscpe, looking at:
single celled organisms,
simpler parts of a complex emergent system. Cell Text:

Inspired by the previous found imagery, I tried applying a visually similar cell material to letterforms using 3d software. Cellular Automata:

Looking into Cellular Automata, a system inspired by the behaviour of complex cell systems in real life. Re created through code using simple rules and if stements. C.A Text:

Treating text and the pixels that make up the form at different resolutions in the same way as the cellular automata systems. The text essentially becoming a collony of individual cells behaving on the defined simple rules. C.A Image:

Treating my found imagery in the same way as the above text examples.
